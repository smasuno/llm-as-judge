## Executive Summary

Based on a systematic analysis of yamaura's financial filings, the Company-Specific Risk Premium (CSRP) is assessed at **150–200 basis points, with a recommended midpoint of 175 bps**, reflecting a composite risk score of **2.98 out of 5.0**. The overall risk profile is best characterized as moderately elevated, driven by a concentrated and cyclically exposed business model that is only partially offset by an exceptionally strong balance sheet. On the risk side, the dominant concern is yamaura's near-total dependence on project-based construction revenue — with the construction segment alone accounting for approximately 80% of consolidated sales (建設事業 28,636,728千円 / 連結売上高 35,613,772千円) — combined with a sharp 21.6% year-on-year decline in order intake (受注高 375億31百万円、前年同期比103億28百万円(21.6％)の減少) and management's own acknowledgment of intensifying price competition (価格競争が依然として激しい状況で推移しております). Structural labor market risk is explicitly flagged in the filing — 建設業界においては、建設技術者・技能労働者が減少傾向であり、高齢化と労働者のさらなる減少が進むと、工期の遅れや人件費の上昇を招き、業績等に影響を及ぼす可能性があります — and key-person concentration around the founding-family president, together with a related-party conflict arising from his concurrent role as representative director of subsidiary ヤマウラ企画開発㈱, adds governance risk. These pressures are meaningfully counterbalanced by yamaura's debt-free balance sheet, a net-cash position of approximately ¥12.15 billion (現金及び現金同等物 12,150,760千円), an interest coverage ratio of approximately 262x, a pension plan now in surplus, and above-average board independence — factors that collectively anchor the premium at the midpoint of the moderate-risk range rather than at its upper bound.

### Risk Driver #2 — Structural Labor Shortage in the Construction Workforce
**Signal:** OPS_08 | Labor and Union Risk | Score: 4

**The Issue:**
Yamaura's operating model is entirely dependent on the availability of skilled construction engineers and craft workers — a resource that the filing explicitly identifies as structurally and irreversibly declining. The risk is not cyclical but demographic: an aging workforce combined with a secular decline in new entrants to the construction trades creates a supply constraint that no individual company can resolve through its own actions alone. The filing discloses no specific mitigation measures — no workforce development programmes, no apprenticeship pipelines, no union agreements, and no automation roadmap sufficient to offset headcount shortfalls.

**Cited Evidence:**
> 建設業界においては、建設技術者・技能労働者が減少傾向であり、高齢化と労働者のさらなる減少が進むと、工期の遅れや人件費の上昇を招き、業績等に影響を及ぼす可能性があります。

**Why It Matters for CSRP:**
The consequences of labor scarcity are twofold and mutually reinforcing: project delays erode client relationships and expose the company to contractual penalties, while rising labor costs compress margins on fixed-price contracts where cost overruns cannot be passed through. While yamaura's early adoption of construction DX — BIMをはじめ３Dレーザースキャナーでの現場環境のデジタル化等IoT技術を駆使 — provides a partial structural offset (OPS_06 scored 2), the technology investment is not disclosed at a scale sufficient to neutralise the workforce risk. ## Methodology Note

### Model and Run Parameters

This CSRP report was generated on **2026-06-06** using model **claude-sonnet-4-6**. The analysis was conducted in **standalone mode** (comparables mode: off), meaning that all signal scores are calibrated against a generalised sector peer anchor rather than against a dynamically constructed peer group drawn from observable market transactions. Where comparables mode is active, signal scores are benchmarked relative to a specific set of identified comparable companies; in its absence, the peer-average anchor of **3.0** represents a hypothetical mid-market construction-sector participant with no distinguishing risk characteristics above or below the norm. Scores above 3.0 indicate above-average risk relative to that anchor; scores below 3.0 indicate below-average risk.

---

### Scoring Architecture

The CSRP framework evaluates company-specific risk across **eight weighted categories**, each populated by a defined universe of granular signals. Individual signals are scored on a **1-to-5 integer scale**, where:

| Score | Risk Characterisation |
|---|---|
| 1 | Materially below peer-average risk |
| 2 | Below peer-average risk |
| 3 | Peer-average risk (neutral anchor) |
| 4 | Above peer-average risk |
| 5 | Materially above peer-average risk |

Category scores are computed as the arithmetic mean of all scored signals within that category. The **composite score** is then derived as a weighted average across the eight categories, producing a single scalar value that maps to a basis-point premium range via the framework's score-to-premium conversion table. For this engagement, yamaura's composite score of **2.98** falls within the **2.50–3.49** band, which corresponds to a CSRP range of **150–200 bps** and a recommended midpoint of **175 bps**.

The score-to-premium mapping is summarised below:

| Composite Score Band | CSRP Range | Recommended Midpoint |
|---|---|---|
| 1.00 – 1.49 | 0 – 50 bps | 25 bps |
| 1.50 – 2.49 | 50 – 100 bps | 75 bps |
| 2.50 – 3.49 | 150 – 200 bps | 175 bps |

### Signal Coverage and Unscored Signals

Of the full signal universe evaluated, **25 signals were left unscored** in this run. Unscored signals are excluded from category averages and do not influence the composite score. They arise for one of three reasons, each of which reflects a data availability constraint rather than an analytical judgement that the underlying risk is absent:

**1. No Supporting Evidence in Selected Sections**
The majority of unscored signals — including REV_01, REV_03, REV_04, REV_05, MGMT_03, MGMT_06, FIN_04, FIN_09, EQ_02, and EQ_03 — could not be scored because the relevant disclosures were not present in the filing sections made available for analysis. Japanese securities filings (有価証券報告書) are structured documents, and the sections selected for ingestion may not capture every disclosure that would be relevant to a given signal. For example, customer concentration data (REV_03), management compensation structure (MGMT_03), and litigation contingencies (FIN_04) are typically disclosed in sections of the filing that were not included in the analytical scope of this run.

**2. Insufficient Time-Series Data**
Signal **REV_06** (multi-year revenue trend) requires a minimum of three years of revenue history to score reliably. A single filing provides at most two periods of comparative data, making it impossible to distinguish a structural trend from a one-period fluctuation. The available data shows revenue of ¥35,613,772k for the current period against the prior period, but without a third data point no statistically meaningful trend score can be assigned.

**3. Missing Specific Financial Inputs**
Signal **FIN_01** (net leverage ratio) could not be scored due to missing inputs required to compute a reliable debt/EBITDA or net debt figure. While the filing confirms a debt-free balance sheet — 長期借入金、リース債務及びその他の有利子負債の連結決算日後の返済予定額 該当事項はありません — the framework requires a complete set of verified inputs before assigning a threshold-based score, and the absence of any gross debt figure prevents the automated threshold check from executing.

### Limitations and Recommended Supplementary Analysis
